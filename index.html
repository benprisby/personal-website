About Me

Welcome! I designed this site to showcase both my technical and non-technical sides, which more often than not intertwine.

I’ve spent the last six years at Desktop Metal building software that powers industrial 3D printers, enabling mass manufacturing of everything from automotive parts to medical devices. What fascinates me most is the challenge of making complex systems feel simple and intuitive.

During my time at DM, my proudest technical achievement is the Maxwell platform: the unified software foundation that lets our small team support an expanding, diverse product portfolio without sacrificing quality (or sanity). I also have a niche for creating embedded user interfaces that make our devices easy to use.

Outside of work, I’m constantly experimenting. My home lab is where I prototype ideas and stay current with technologies that pique my interest. I’ve been playing guitar since I was nine and it continues to be my favorite creative outlet, where I explore patterns and emotions through songwriting. And I’ve developed a passion for mixology; there’s something deeply satisfying about balancing flavors and perfecting techniques. When I’m not crafting cocktails or diving into code, my wife and I are usually training our puppy Luna.

Whether I’m architecting software, crafting a cocktail, or working out a chord progression, I’m drawn to the intersection of technical precision and creative expression. The best solutions often come from understanding both the system and the human experience within it.
